# Quantum Computing: The End of Encryption?

The race to build a practical quantum computer isn't just about scientific achievement—it's about who will control the future of information security. When quantum computers reach sufficient scale, they will break virtually all current encryption methods.

## The Quantum Threat

Traditional computers use bits (0 or 1). Quantum computers use **qubits** that can be both simultaneously (superposition), enabling exponential computational power.

### What Quantum Computers Can Break

Current encryption methods vulnerable to quantum attacks:

- **RSA encryption**: Used for HTTPS, email, VPNs
- **Elliptic Curve Cryptography**: Used in Bitcoin, Signal
- **Diffie-Hellman**: Used for key exchange
- **DSA**: Used for digital signatures

## Post-Quantum Cryptography

The race to develop quantum-resistant encryption:

### NIST's Post-Quantum Standards

In 2024, NIST selected quantum-resistant algorithms:

- **CRYSTALS-Kyber**: Key encapsulation
- **CRYSTALS-Dilithium**: Digital signatures
- **SPHINCS+**: Hash-based signatures
- **FALCON**: Lattice-based signatures

